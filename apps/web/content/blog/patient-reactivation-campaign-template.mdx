# Patient Reactivation Campaign Template: The Exact Script That Books 14% of Dormant Patients

Most dental and MedSpa practices sit on a goldmine they never mine: their existing patient database.

The average practice has 400–800 patients who were seen at least once but haven't returned in 12+ months. These are people who already trust you, already have your file, and already experienced your service. The only question is: why haven't they come back?

The answer, 80% of the time, is simple: **no one asked them to.**

## What Is a Patient Reactivation Campaign?

A patient reactivation campaign is a structured outreach sequence — via phone, SMS, and email — that systematically contacts your dormant patient list with a specific, personalized reason to return.

The key word is *specific*. "We miss you, come back!" doesn't work. What works is:

> *"Hi, this is calling from [Clinic Name]. We noticed it's been about 14 months since your last cleaning with Dr. Johnson. We have availability on Thursday at 2 PM or Friday morning — would either of those work for you?"*

The difference: one is generic marketing, one is a specific, time-sensitive offer with a real appointment pre-loaded.

## The Exact Campaign Template (Copy This)

### Phase 1: AI Voice Call (Day 1)

**Script for a dental practice:**

> "Hi, may I speak with [Patient Name]? 
> 
> This is [AI Name] calling on behalf of [Practice Name]. I'm reaching out because it's been a little over a year since your last visit with us, and Dr. [Name] wanted to make sure we stayed connected.
> 
> We have a few openings coming up for routine cleanings this week and next — it only takes about 45 minutes and most insurance covers it fully. 
> 
> Would Thursday at 2 PM or Friday at 10 AM work for you?"

**If they say no:**
> "No worries at all! Would it be helpful if I sent you a text link so you can pick a time that works better for your schedule?"

**If they say yes:**
> "Perfect. I'm booking you in for [day/time] with Dr. [Name] at [address]. You'll get a confirmation text in just a moment. Is [phone number] still the best number for your reminder?"

### Phase 2: SMS Follow-Up (24 Hours Later if No Answer)

```
[Practice Name]: Hi [First Name], we tried to reach you yesterday about your annual cleaning. 

Dr. [Name] has Thursday at 2 PM or Friday at 10 AM available — both are currently open.

Reply YES to confirm one of these slots, or tap here to pick your own time: [booking link]

Reply STOP to opt out.
```

### Phase 3: Final SMS (Day 5 if No Response)

```
[Practice Name]: Last chance — we're releasing these slots today. 

[First Name], we'd love to see you back. Book anytime here: [booking link]

Reply STOP to stop messages.
```

## Timing and Cadence

Based on data across our client deployments, the optimal reactivation sequence is:

| Day | Touch | Channel | Notes |
|-----|-------|---------|-------|
| Day 1 | Initial outreach | AI Voice Call | Between 10 AM–12 PM or 5 PM–7 PM local time |
| Day 1 | Voicemail drop | Voice VM | If no answer — natural, brief, personal tone |
| Day 2 | First follow-up | SMS | Send at 11 AM local time |
| Day 5 | Final touch | SMS | Lower urgency, release-the-slot framing |

Do not contact anyone on Mondays before 10 AM or after 8 PM local time. Compliance matters.

## Segmentation: Not All Dormant Patients Are Equal

Before you blast your entire database, segment it:

**Tier 1 — High Priority (contact first)**
- Patients seen 12–18 months ago (still warm, easiest reactivation)
- Patients with insurance that renews January 1 or July 1 (timing angle)
- Patients who had unfinished treatment plans

**Tier 2 — Medium Priority**
- Patients seen 18–36 months ago
- Patients without outstanding dental concerns noted

**Tier 3 — Low Priority / Clean List**
- Patients not seen in 3+ years
- Patients who previously requested no contact

Start with Tier 1 only. You'll typically see a **14–18% reactivation rate** in this group during the first campaign.
