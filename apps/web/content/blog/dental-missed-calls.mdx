# The True Cost of a Missed Call for Dental Practices

Your phone rings. Your front desk is checking in a patient, processing insurance, and explaining post-op care simultaneously. The call goes to voicemail.

That caller? They needed an emergency appointment for tooth pain. They waited 10 seconds, hung up, and Googled the next dentist on the list. **You just lost a patient worth $3,200 in lifetime value.**

## The Numbers Don't Lie

Research from the Dental Economics report shows:

| Metric | Industry Average |
|--------|-----------------|
| Calls that go unanswered during business hours | 32% |
| After-hours calls (evenings/weekends) | 100% missed |
| Callers who leave voicemails | 20% |
| Voicemail callers who actually get called back same-day | 45% |
| Patients who call a competitor after no answer | 85% |

For a practice receiving **60 calls per week**, that's roughly **19 missed calls** — and at an average new patient value of $450 for the first visit alone, you're hemorrhaging **$8,500/week** in potential revenue.

## The After-Hours Gap Is Even Worse

35% of dental appointment requests happen outside business hours — evenings, weekends, and holidays. Every single one of those calls currently goes to a voicemail that says "We'll call you back during business hours."

By then, they've already booked with someone else.
